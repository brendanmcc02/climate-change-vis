Analytics - Asia

All three of Tourism, Rainfall and Temperature all experience very similar movements from 2007 - 2010. During that period, all three statistics drop. However, as the tourism starts to rise from 2009, so does the rainfall at that time. There is also a slight correlatiion between rainfall and tourism from 2012 onwards. We can see that both statistics are very gradually increasing during this period.


Out of all the graphs, Japan has to be one of the most interesting as it has a extremely noticeable boom in tourism from 2011 onwards. But what I found most interesting was that, from my analysis of the other countries, when a countries' tourism starts to rise, this would normally incur an increase in Temperature or it would make the Temperature more inconstent. The opposite is seen in Japan. As the Tourism starts to sky-rocket, the rainfall becomes very consitent during this period, almost making a straight line. I think this is because Japan has, in recent years, placed a great emphasis on renewable energy as opposed to its Asian counterpart, China.
