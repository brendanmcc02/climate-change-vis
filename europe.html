Analytics - Europe

Unlike Ireland, there is an immediate correlation between Rainfall and Temperature here. You can see that the Temperature and Rainfall behave in almost the exact same manner from 1995 - 1997, the same also occurs from 2009 - 2011. I think the reason why the UK has obvious correlation between Rainfall and Temperature, while Ireland does not, is because the UK is about as twice as big in size as Ireland. Hence, the temperature in the UK is less consistent. There is also a slight correlation between rainfall and tourism in 2001. The tourism in the UK really starts to rise in 2001, and similarly, the rainfall experiences a great rise in the same year. Moreover, in 2005 the rainfall starts to gradually rise until 2008. This growth almost exactly mirrors the steady rise in tourism from 2005 - 2008.


France is the most popular Tourist Destination as of today. Similar to the UK, there is an obvious correlation between rainfall and Temperature. They both experience very similar movements from 2008 onwards. However, there is a noticeable correlation between tourism and rainfall. From 1995 - 2001, both the rainfall and tourism increase steadily at the same rate. Further more, the tourism starts to steadily decrease from 2007 - 2010. During this time the rainfall also experiences a similar decline. Although, after this decline the tourism starts to increase again in 2010. At this time, the rainfall also starts to increase. It is also worth noting that the national temperature is much less consistent that it was in the 1990s. From 2010 onwards, the temperature spikes up and down, as opposed to the consistent nature of the temperature from 1995 - 2008.


In a similar fashion to France, there is a visible correlation between tourism and temperature. As you can see, the tourism in spain starts to increase steadily from 2007. From 2005 (just 2 years earlier), the temperature becomes much more inconsistent. For example, the temperature from 1995 - 2005 is almost a straight line, whereas the temperature is much more inconsistent from 2005 onwards (when the tourism in Spain really started to kick off). Unlike France however, you can see that in 2010 the tourism almost doubles. From 2010 onwards, the rainfall decreases quite drastically. I found this interesting as it possibly suggested that the decline in rainfall led to an increase in tourism in Spain.
